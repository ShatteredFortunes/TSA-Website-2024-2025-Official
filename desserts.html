    A sponge cake soaked in three types of milk (condensed milk and evaporated milk), topped with whipped cream.
    Fun Fact: The name translates to "Three Milks Cake", and the combination allow it to have its unique texture and flavor. It is soaked in liquid to preserve and/or moisten it.

    A dense and creamy frozen dessert made with milk, sugar, and spices like cardamom and saffron.
    Fun Fact: It was believed to be served to royalty in the Mughal Empire, and the slow cooking process of kulfi provides its rich, dense, and creamy flavors and textures.

    FLAN

    A caramel custard dessert with a silky smooth texture.
    Fun Fact: It used to be more of a savory dessert, and the word "flan" came from the French word "flaon", which comes from the Latin word "flado", meaning flat cake or custard.

    Contains: dairy, eggs


    SESAME BALLS

    A crisp and crunchy exterior that reveals a chewy and exquisite filling.
    Fun Fact: Sesame balls are very popular in China, where they have many names. They can contain a variety of fillings such as pastes like red beans. Their golden color and round shape are lucky, the sweetiness showcases happiness, and the fact that they expand many times in size is a metaphor that states that some ventures will bring back large returns and fortunes.
